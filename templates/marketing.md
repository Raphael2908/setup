## The strategy at a glance

Channels in priority order:
1. **Direct outreach to the ICP** — the actual source of the first <N>; founder-led, hand-to-hand.
2. **Dogfood content engine** — on-brand content the product makes about itself; reused as outreach +
   landing proof + paid creatives. One production effort, several uses.
3. **Small paid test** (~few hundred $) — only after organic shows which creative converts.

Plus an always-on SEO blog and a tightened conversion funnel underneath.

## CHANNEL 2 — Dogfood content engine (free, start now)
Make a steady stream of on-brand content and post daily. The meta-hook: "<made with <PRODUCT>>". Reuse
the best as outreach demos, landing social proof, and paid creatives.

## CHANNEL 3 — Small paid test (~few hundred $)
Don't spend until Channel 2 shows which creative resonates. Take the top organic piece and run it as the
cheapest validated paid loop for the category. Treat the budget as learning spend.

## Always-on: SEO blog (background compounding)
File-based blog (`frontend/content/blog/`). ~1 bottom-funnel post/week on high-intent terms. House
style: <UK/US English, no em dashes, honest about gaps, no competitor pricing claims>.

## Conversion: make the live product convert the traffic you earn
- **Deliver the aha early** (before signup if there's no free trial).
- **Signup → first paid action frictionless** — pre-seed example data so the first run needs zero setup.
- **Social proof on the landing page** — real output, framed honestly.
- **Analytics funnel** (signup → paid → activated) so attribution and the paid test aren't blind.

## Sequenced timeline
- **Week 0:** conversion basics + first dogfood content + ICP list + tracking sheet.
- **Week 1:** daily content + <N>/day personalised outreach (Channel 1 priority); 1 SEO post.
- **Week 2:** scale outreach to the replying segment; collect testimonials.
- **Week 3–4:** deploy the paid test on the best organic creative; double down on the converting segment.
